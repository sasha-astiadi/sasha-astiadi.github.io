About me

More than just UX, I see myself as a Human-centered designer. It is my duty and aspiration to define the ways in which humans relate to and interact with the world, how we can navigate space effectively, and how we are able to get our needs and demands met in this increasingly product-focused world.

I firmly believe in the application of empathical approaches and psychological principles to the design and engineering of products, processes, and systems in order to increase productivity, safety, satisfaction and comfort with a specific focus on the interaction between human and the product of interest.
