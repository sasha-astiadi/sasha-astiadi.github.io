Genesis

UX Design Case Study
Posted by Sasha Astiadi on September 20, 2018

Time: September 2018
Platform: Web responsive App
UX Tools used: Axure RP, Figma, Adobe XD

Genesis is a team organization tool that seeks collective agreement within team members in by adopting design-thinking method; scoring collective ideas based on pre-determined criteria. Genesis enable users to save time, minimize effort, get precisely calculated outcomes, and keep all team decisions together..


Range of Work
  • Affinity Mapping
  • User Personas
  • User Task Flows

How

Team members would be able to propose project ideas, that would later on be rated based on criteria. By using computer algorithm and based on the data provided by team members, Genesis would show users the rating result and the idea with the highest score.


What I learned

While this project existed in the academic and conceptual space of CODE university, we very much treated it as a real project given by a client in a professional manner. Our realistic view did not limit our creativityâ€”it maximized it. My team took a leap beyond what was typical. We wanted to follow our research with fidelity, so when a real case could be made for clients, we would take advantage of our learning process. I wanted to provide our prospective users with the best experience possible. Genesis turned out to be fairly satisfying, both in look and in concept, a product result reflecting our hard effort that we are all very proud of.
